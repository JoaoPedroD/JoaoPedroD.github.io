abstract: "This work presents a novel systematic methodology to analyse the capabilities and limitations of Large Language Models (LLMs) with feedback from a formal inference engine, on logic theory induction. We use a complexity-graded approach based on rule dependency structure to quantify specific inference challenges, and integrate LLMs with formal methods to enhance inference control and explainability in natural language processing. Our empirical findings show that the most advanced LLMs can compete with state-of-the-art Inductive Logic Programming systems, though they struggle more with tracking extended predicate relationship chains than with managing overall theory complexity."
